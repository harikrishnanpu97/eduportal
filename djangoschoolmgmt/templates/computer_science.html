Computer Science Department

Greenwood University

Bachelor's Course Structure

  • Duration: 3-4 years (depending on the program structure).

  • Semester Start: Usually in August or September.
  • Introduction to Computer Science
  • Data Structures and Algorithms
  • Computer Architecture
  • Operating Systems
  • Database Management Systems
  • Software Engineering
  • Computer Networks
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Machine Learning
  • Cybersecurity
  • Web Development

Master's Course Structure

  • Duration: 1-2 years (Masters) or 3-5 years (Ph.D.).

  • Semester Start: Typically in August or September for Masters programs; Ph.D. programs may have rolling admissions.
  • Advanced Algorithms
  • Computer Vision
  • Big Data Analytics
  • Cloud Computing
  • Internet of Things (IoT)
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Software Development Methodologies

Diploma Course Structure

  • Duration: 1-2 years (Masters) or 3-5 years (Ph.D.).

  • Semester Start: Typically in August or September for Masters programs; Ph.D. programs may have rolling admissions.
  • Advanced Research Topics in Computer Science
  • Dissertation Proposal in Computer Science
  • Specialized Topics in Computer Science
  • Ethics in Computing
  • Teaching Practicum in Computer Science
  • Dissertation Research and Writing in Computer Science

Doctorate (Ph.D) Course Structure

  • Duration: Typically 3-5 years (may vary based on research and dissertation work).
  • Semester Start: Rolling admissions, often with ongoing research projects.
  • Advanced Research Methodologies in Computer Science
  • Literature Review in Computer Science
  • Advanced Programming Techniques
  • Grant Writing and Funding in Computer Science
  • Specialized Seminars in Computer Science Topics
  • Dissertation Research and Writing in Computer Science

Foundation Course Structure

  • Duration: Varies based on the program, typically 1 year.
  • Semester Start: Generally aligned with undergraduate intake, often in August or September.
  • Introduction to Computer Science Concepts
  • Basic Mathematics for Computer Science
  • Introduction to Programming
  • Fundamentals of Data Structures
  • Computer Networking Basics
  • Introduction to Software Engineering
  • Database Management Fundamentals
  • Web Development Basics

Scholarships and Financial Aid

Greenwood University offers scholarships and financial aid programs for eligible Computer Science students. Reach out to the university for more details on how to apply and avail of these opportunities to support your academic journey in computer science.
